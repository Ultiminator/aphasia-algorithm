Aphasia Algorithm

The first 3 questions are required and are based on Boston Classification, while the last 3 questions are optional for further assessment of more language impairment only if the first 3 components are intact.
Boston Classification

Is the patient fluent?

The patient can express themselves without paraphasia, agramatism, paragramatism, reduced prosody, or recurrent utterence?

Does the patient comprehend?

Your patient clearly understands what is being said to Them?

Can the patient repeat?

The person can repeat words or phrases?
Additional Assessment

Intact naming?

The patient can name objects in front of them or concepts in their head without word finding difficulty?

The patient reads fluently?

This is of no value if there is already comprehension or visual impairment, or the patient is illiterate.

The patient writes correctly?

This is of no value if there is already weakness or visual impairment, or the patient is illiterate.
This algorithm is designed to help in identifying the type of language impairment and is based on the Boston Classification of Aphasia and additional information from the book "CURRENT Diagnosis & Treatment: Neurology, 3e" for John C.M. Brust. Designed and developed by Ahmed Salah.
