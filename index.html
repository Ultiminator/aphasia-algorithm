Aphasia Algorithm

The first 3 questions are required and are based on Boston Classification, while the last 3 questions are optional for further assessment of more language impairment only if the first 3 components are intact.
Boston Classification

Is the patient fluent?

Intact expression; No paraphasia, agramatism, paragramatism, or reduced prosody

Can the patient comprehend?

the patient comprehends spoken messages

Intact repetition?

can the person repeat words or phrases?
Additional Assessment

can the patient name objects or concepts?

the patient doesn't have difficulty in finding the names of objects or concepts

Fluent reading?

This is of no value if the comprehension is already impaired or the patient is illiterate

Can the patien write?

Ignore if there is weakness or the patient is already illiterate
This algorithm is designed to help in identifying the type of language impairment and is based on the Boston Classification of Aphasia and additional information from the book "CURRENT Diagnosis & Treatment: Neurology, 3e" for John C.M. Brust. Designed and developed by Ahmed Salah.
